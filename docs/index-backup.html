Hello, I'm Adam Kiryk, a front end developer and designer

I work at the intersection of user experience, visual design, and code, and I focus on creating beautiful, well-crafted, and accessible websites — and I'm always passionate about usability, maintainability, and performance.

skills

code

I work in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — most recently creating components in React with Redux for an NPR content editing tool — and I use PHP to build themes in Drupal and Wordpress. I use modern workflow tools like Webpack and Gulp; my preferred CSS preprocessor is SASS; and I use git for version control. Mostly self-taught, I completed the Udacity front end developer certificate program in 2016.

ux + design

I studied interaction design at the California College of the Arts in San Francisco and Oakland, and have expertise in digital product design, user experience, web design, and branding. As a senior product designer at NPR over the past six years, I’ve honed my skills in design thinking, user research, prototyping, user testing, and designing clean and usable visual systems.

communication

With years of experience presenting work to clients, colleagues, and stakeholders, I’m skilled at speaking in front of groups, explaining the rationale behind design decisions, and helping my colleagues discuss challenges and work out technical or design solutions. I’m also a capable writer, both in documenting work for colleagues and in blogging about work for the public.

collaboration

I’m experienced working with multidisciplinary teams of designers, developers, user researchers, product managers, and others, and I have a successful track record of resolving differences on my team and helping my colleagues stay focused and productive.

work

I worked for NPR from 2011 to 2017, doing product design, user experience, and front end development on websites and web applications used by NPR member stations. Before that, I worked at Arnold Worldwide, where I created websites and digital campaigns for brands like Progressive Insurance, RadioShack, and Ocean Spray.
